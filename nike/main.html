About The NIKE PROJECT

NIKE: Just do it The Nike Shopping Cart is a crucial component of the brand's e-commerce platform, designed to provide users with a seamless and efficient shopping experience. This digital cart serves as a virtual repository for selected products, allowing users to easily add, review, and manage their chosen items before proceeding to checkout. With a focus on usability and mobile responsiveness, the Nike Shopping Cart ensures that users can effortlessly navigate through the e-commerce interface, making it convenient to explore and finalize their purchase decisions. The shopping cart integrates visually appealing design elements, making it visually clear and user-friendly. Furthermore, it may incorporate personalized recommendations, loyalty program integration, and efficient error handling to enhance user satisfaction and streamline the entire shopping journey. Through these features, the Nike Shopping Cart aims to optimize the user experience, encouraging smooth transactions and fostering post-purchase engagement.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Online shoppers often face a lack of interactive features that allow them to visualize products in different styles and colors, leading to uncertainty in their choices and a decrease in customer satisfaction and sales.

GOAL

The objective of the Nike shopping product UI/UX design is to enhance the online shopping experience by providing customers with an interactive and immersive interface. This should allow them to visualize the products in various colors, select sizes easily, and make purchasing decisions confidently.

Research

Research Goal

The research aimed to analyze and improve the Nike shopping cart user experience, focusing on optimizing functionality, usability, and overall satisfaction. The goal was to identify potential pain points, enhance the efficiency of the shopping cart, and provide valuable insights for refining the app's design to boost user engagement

Asking the right Questions

Usability and Efficiency:

  • How user-friendly and efficient is the current Nike shopping cart interface in facilitating the addition, removal, and modification of items?
  • What are the common usability challenges users encounter while interacting with the shopping cart?

    • User Journey:

  • What is the typical user journey within the shopping cart, from adding items to the final checkout process?
  • Are there specific stages within the shopping cart experience where users tend to drop off, and if so, why?

    • Visual Design and Clarity:

  • How visually clear and appealing is the presentation of items within the shopping cart?
  • Do users find it easy to review and confirm their selected items before proceeding to checkout?

    • Proposed Solution

    The solution is an intuitive and dynamic product display interface that allows users to interact with the carousel to view shoes in multiple colors. The background adapts to the color of the shoe selected, providing a consistent visual theme and aiding in the visualization of the product in different contexts.

    Functional Requirements

  • Interactive carousel for displaying shoe colors.
  • Dynamic background color changes to match the selected shoe.
  • Responsive size selection options.
  • Integration of e-commerce functionalities, such as add to cart and favorites.
  • Real-time updates to inventory and sizes available.
  • User account integration for personalized experiences.

    • Non Functional Requirements

  • High-performance interface to ensure smooth carousel interaction.
  • Adaptive design for various screen sizes and devices.
  • Aesthetically pleasing animations and transitions.
  • Secure and efficient handling of user data and payment information.
  • Accessibility features for all types of users

    • Design Considerations

  • The UI must prioritize ease of use, allowing users to navigate through color options effortlessly.
  • The UX should be engaging, with the background color change providing instant visual feedback.
  • The design should be clean and minimalistic, keeping the focus on the products.
  • The system should be optimized for quick loading times, despite the dynamic content.
