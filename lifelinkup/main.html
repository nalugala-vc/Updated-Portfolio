PROBLEM STATEMENT

Organ transplantation, a beacon of hope for countless individuals, faces significant challenges in efficient and timely donor-recipient matching. The existing processes are often cumbersome, leading to delays that can be critical, especially for those facing life-threatening conditions. This inefficiency can result in missed opportunities for life-saving organ transplants, contributing to increased suffering and loss.

GOAL

To design a mobile application that aims to streamline the organ donation process, ensuring swift and efficient matches to save more lives through a user-friendly digital platform.

Research

Research Goal

To comprehensively understand the current challenges and pain points in the organ donation process, as well as to gather insights into the needs and preferences of potential organ donors and recipients. This research served as the foundation for informing the design and development of Life LinkUp, ensuring a user-centric and effective solution to bridge the gaps in the organ transplantation journey.

Asking the right Questions

What are the primary challenges and inefficiencies faced in the current organ donation and transplantation process?
How do potential organ donors and recipients currently navigate and perceive the organ donation journey?
What information sources do individuals rely on when considering organ donation, and how can these be optimized for clarity and accessibility?
What emotional and practical considerations influence the decision-making process for both donors and recipients in the organ transplantation journey?
What are the existing pain points and barriers that may discourage individuals from participating in organ donation programs?
What features and functionalities would potential donors and recipients value in a digital platform aiming to streamline and enhance the organ donation process?
How can the organ donation process be made more inclusive and accessible to a diverse range of individuals?
How can technology be leveraged to address the informational and emotional needs of individuals involved in organ donation, making the process more transparent and supportive?
What are the key concerns and expectations regarding privacy and security when engaging with a digital platform for organ donation?

The audience

  • Organ Donors:Individuals who are willing to donate organs and want a streamlined and user-friendly platform to express their intent and go through the donation process.
  • Organ Recipients:Individuals who are in need of organ transplants and seek a platform that facilitates efficient matching with potential donors.
  • Families and Supporters:Loved ones and supporters of both donors and recipients who may play a role in the decision-making process and require information and support.
  • Medical Professionals:Healthcare professionals involved in the organ transplantation process, such as transplant coordinators and surgeons, who may use the app to facilitate and coordinate donations.
  • Organ Procurement Organizations (OPOs) and Transplant Centers:Institutions and organizations responsible for managing and facilitating organ donations may use the app to enhance their operations and improve communication with donors and recipients.

    Proposed Solution

    Functional Requirements

  • User authentication system for sign-up and sign-in.
  • Educational content on the home page about organ donation.
  • Search functionality for users to find specific information within the app.
  • Form pages for new requests, capturing personal information, emergency contact details, medical history, and necessary medical markers such as blood type and HLA typing.
  • AI chatbot for real-time user assistance.
  • Profile management for users to update their personal information.
  • Notifications system to alert users about matches and events.

    • Non Functional Requirements

  • Data privacy and security measures to protect sensitive personal and medical information.
  • Scalable architecture to support a growing user base and data volume.
  • Reliable back-end systems to ensure uninterrupted service.
  • Intuitive and responsive design across various devices and screen sizes.

    • Design Considerations

  • A design language that conveys trust and confidentiality, using purple as a calming and distinguished color.
  • Clear and accessible forms for data entry, minimizing user fatigue and potential errors.
  • The AI assistant should have a friendly persona to make the interaction more comfortable.
  • A strong focus on data security, with transparent user consent mechanisms.
  • User experience tailored to demystify and destigmatize the organ donation process.
